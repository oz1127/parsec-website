Plasma is a proposed framework for incentivized and enforced execution of smart contracts which is scalable to a significant amount of state updates per second (potentially billions) enabling the blockchain to be able to represent a significant amount of decentralized financial applications worldwide. You can read more about it here: https://plasma.io/
Plasma will get rid of unnecessary data in the root chain. It will handle smart contracts much like its foundation, but will only broadcast completed transactions to the public Ethereum chain. This saves an enormous amount of processing power and memory for the chain’s participants, making it more inexpensive to interact with the system’s other participants. It will also speed up transactions enough to let decentralized apps run without worrying about backlog.
PARSEC chain is fast, secure and scalable. It’s based on Plasma and Proof of stake (PoS) and allows users to make many fast and cheap transactions.
Proof of stake (PoS) is a type of algorithm by which a cryptocurrency blockchain network aims to achieve distributed consensus.
Proof of work (PoW) requires proof that work of some kind occurred. In the case of Ethereum miners are required to do this work before any of their blocks is accepted by others. Proof of stake (PoS) requires users that have a high stake at the currency (i.e. hold a lot of tokens) to determine the next block. The main difference could be summarized in that PoW requires burning an external resource (mining hardware) while PoS does not. PoS can be considered to be the most environmentally friendly and cost-effective consensus method, but has a number of drawbacks that pioneers such as the Ethereum development team and our team are attempting to solve.
Stake - an amount of PSC token locked in the PARSEC contract for a specific slot. The stake is used as a collateral to vouch for the correctness of submitted block. Staking - is the "mining" of PoS (proof of stake). It simply stands for holding a token in your wallet for a fixed period, then earning interest on it. The reward that one earns from staking varies depending on the length of the time that they hold it. The longer the stake duration, the higher the returns. Epoch - is a period of x blocks on the PARSEC chain. After each epoch slots can change ownership and the tax rate is increased or decreased. Per epoch each slot will become a slot leader at least once.
Validator - is an individual or organization operating nodes and having stakes.

If you want to become our validator, your steps are following:
  1. Download the node software: https://validator.parseclabs.org/
  2. Install and start the node. During the first start the signer-key is created. Note the signer-address.
  3. Wait for the node to completely sync the chain.
  4. Fund the signer-address with enough Ether to pay for block submission transactions.
  5. Pick a slot for use by the validator. Prepare enough funds on a secure wallet (cold storage or multi-sig). Deposit funds from the secure wallet to the validator slot, passing in the signer-address.
  6. The node software will notice the deposit and start proposing blocks within 2 to 3 epochs.

It's obvious that every action which can earn profit to its executor have some risks behind it, otherwise this action delivers zero profit. We have captured these risks, which can affect profits of validators:
  • Slashing Risk - Submission of invalid blocks / submission of two blocks with the same height.
  • Coordination Risk - Increase of base tax due to low proposal rate of Validators (Collective penalty).
  • Market Risk - Low PSC market valuation / rising Ether gas prices consume validators’ profits.
  • Centralization Risk - Effect of team reputation affecting the network value.
  • Regulatory Risk - Various laws affecting validators and/or users of network.
